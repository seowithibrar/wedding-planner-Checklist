# WeddingPlanningChecklists.org – Universal Blog Post Design & Content Rules

## Purpose
These rules apply to **every blog post** on WeddingPlanningChecklists.org, including existing articles and all future content. The goal is to create a consistent, premium, SEO-friendly, and user-focused reading experience across the entire website.

## 1. Consistent Blog Structure
Every article must follow this order:
1. Breadcrumb Navigation
2. Featured Hero Image
3. Category Badge
4. H1 Title
5. Article Metadata (Author, Updated Date, Reading Time)
6. Short Introduction (2–4 paragraphs)
7. Quick Summary / Key Takeaways
8. Sticky Table of Contents
9. Main Content
10. FAQ Section
11. Conclusion
12. Related Articles
13. Newsletter / Download CTA
14. Author Box

Never change this structure.

## 2. Typography Rules
* Only one H1 per page.
* H2 for primary sections.
* H3 for subsections.
* H4 only when necessary.
* Keep headings descriptive and keyword-focused.
* Paragraphs should be 2–4 sentences.
* Avoid long text blocks.
* Maintain generous spacing between sections.

## 3. Introduction Rules
Every introduction should:
* Clearly explain the topic.
* Address the reader's intent.
* Mention what the article covers.
* Encourage readers to continue.
Target length: 150–250 words.

## 4. Table of Contents
Every article with more than three H2 sections must include a Table of Contents.
Requirements:
* Sticky on desktop.
* Collapsible on mobile.
* Highlight the active section while scrolling.
* Enable smooth scrolling.

## 5. Image Rules
Every article should include:
* One featured image.
* One supporting image every 300–500 words.
* Descriptive alt text.
* Responsive sizing.
* Lazy loading.
* Rounded corners.
* Consistent image style.
Avoid using repetitive stock photography.

## 6. Content Block Rules
Use a mix of reusable content blocks to improve readability.
Available blocks: Tip Box, Expert Advice, Checklist, Timeline, Warning Box, Quick Facts, Quote, Comparison Table, Step Cards, Key Takeaways, Frequently Asked Questions.
Do not repeat the same block excessively.

## 7. Readability Rules
* Write in short paragraphs.
* Use bullet lists where appropriate.
* Keep sentence length varied.
* Avoid large walls of text.
* Break up content with visuals and callout boxes.

## 8. Internal Linking Rules
Every article must include:
* 5–10 contextual internal links.
* Links to pillar pages.
* Links to related guides.
* Links to relevant checklists and planning tools.
Avoid placing all internal links at the end of the article.

## 9. External Linking Rules
Only link to trusted, authoritative sources when they add value. (Government resources, Official organizations, Industry studies, Reputable wedding vendors). Open external links in a new tab.

## 10. SEO Rules
Every article must include:
* Unique H1.
* SEO-friendly title.
* Optimized meta description.
* Descriptive URL.
* Keyword-rich headings.
* Image alt text.
* FAQ schema (where applicable).
* Article schema.
* Breadcrumb schema.
Avoid keyword stuffing.

## 11. FAQ Rules
Every article should end with 5–10 frequently asked questions addressing common concerns and long-tail search queries.

## 12. Conclusion Rules
The conclusion should:
* Summarize the main points.
* Reinforce key takeaways.
* Encourage readers to take the next step.
* Link to related resources.

## 13. Related Content
Display 3–6 related articles after the conclusion.
Each card should include: Featured image, Category, Article title, Reading time.

## 14. Call-to-Action Rules
Every article must include at least one relevant CTA (Download checklist, Access timeline, Join newsletter, Explore guides). CTAs should feel helpful, not intrusive.

## 15. Mobile Experience
Every article must:
* Be mobile-first.
* Use responsive images.
* Maintain readable font sizes.
* Avoid horizontal scrolling.
* Include large tap targets.
* Keep navigation simple.

## 16. Accessibility
Every blog post should:
* Use semantic HTML.
* Include descriptive image alt text.
* Support keyboard navigation.
* Maintain sufficient color contrast.
* Use accessible form labels.

## 17. Performance
Optimize every article for: Fast loading speed, Lazy-loaded images, Minimal layout shift (CLS), Optimized image formats, Lightweight CSS and JavaScript.

## 18. Content Quality Standards
Answer intent completely, be accurate, include actionable advice, demonstrate expertise, conversational English, avoid duplicate/thin content.

## 19. Visual Consistency
Maintain design language: uniform spacing, typography, reusable blocks, button styles, cards, colors, wedding-inspired visuals.

## 20. Universal Rule
These standards are mandatory for every blog post. Any updates or future content must preserve this structure while improving readability, SEO, and performance.
