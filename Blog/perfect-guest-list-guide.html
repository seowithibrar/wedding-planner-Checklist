Mastering Your Wedding Guest List: The Seating Chart Guide

Updated June 2026
7 Min Read
Evera Editorial

Guide Sections

1. The "A-List" & "B-List" Rules 2. How to Track RSVPs 3. Seating Chart Layouts 4. Family Expectations

01. The "A-List" and "B-List" Strategy

Creating a guest list can be emotionally challenging. To manage count limits and accommodate everyone fairly, divide your guest list into two tiers:

  • A-List: Family members, close friends, and absolutely essential guests who must be present. Send their invitations first.
  • B-List: Colleagues, childhood friends, and extended family. Send invitations to B-list guests as soon as RSVPs (regrets) from A-list guests arrive.

02. How to Track RSVPs Effectively

Set your RSVP deadline exactly 4 to 6 weeks before your wedding date. This gives you ample time to follow up with non-responders and provide a final headcount to your venue and caterer (which is typically due 2 weeks before). Leverage our online Guest List Manager to instantly track acceptances, regrets, meal choices, and dietary constraints.

03. Seating Chart Best Practices

Assigning seats is crucial for maintaining flow and ensuring everyone has a pleasant dining experience. Group guests by relationship (e.g. college friends, maternal family, partner's coworkers) to spark natural conversations. Keep children near their parents, and seat older family members slightly further away from the DJ speakers or band.

04. Managing Family Expectations

If parents are contributing financially to the wedding, they will likely want to invite some of their own friends. Establish clear guest allocations from the very beginning. For example, give 50% of the guest list to you and your partner, and split the remaining 50% between both families. Setting boundaries early prevents seating stress later.
